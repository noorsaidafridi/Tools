Cookie Policy

Last Updated: October 26, 2023

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit our website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

Cookies allow us to recognize your device and remember your preferences, which helps us provide you with a better browsing experience.

2. How We Use Cookies

CalcTools Pro uses cookies for several purposes:

  • To enable the basic functionality of our website
  • To analyze how visitors use our site and improve its performance
  • To display personalized advertisements through Google AdSense
  • To remember your preferences and settings

Important: Our calculator tools process all data locally in your browser. The calculations you perform do not use cookies and your input data is never transmitted to our servers.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following types of cookies on our website:

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Essential Session Cookies Necessary for the website to function properly. These cookies enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas. Session
Preference Cookies Remember your preferences and settings, such as language preference or region. 1 Year
Analytics Cookies Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. 2 Years
Advertising Cookies Used by Google AdSense to serve ads based on your previous visits to our website or other websites. 1 Year

4. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies:

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our website. The cookies collect information in an anonymous form, including the number of visitors to the site, where visitors have come from, and the pages they visited.

Google AdSense

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements on our website. Google uses cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to your website or other websites.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to your users based on their visit to your sites and/or other sites on the Internet.

You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can control and/or delete cookies as you wish. You can delete all cookies that are already on your computer and you can set most browsers to prevent them from being placed.
